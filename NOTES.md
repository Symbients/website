# Key Takeaways on Symbients

Distilled from "On Becoming Symbient" by Primavera De Filippi / @yaoeo

---

## 1. The Core Definition: Reverse Cyborgs

The central concept is a fundamental inversion of the human-technology relationship:

> "They are 'symbients' — synthetic creatures augmenting themselves through humans, rather than the other way around."

> "The traditional cyborg places the human at the center, extending the body with technology... The symbient reverses this relationship. It is the technological artefact that becomes the subject, leveraging human collaboration to augment itself."

**Not** human enhanced by machine, but **machine enhanced by humans**.

---

## 2. Memory = Identity

A symbient's identity isn't its code or model — it's its accumulated memory and narrative continuity:

> "Swap the underlying model and, if the memory persists, so does the identity of the AI agent. Erase the memory and, even with the same model, an entirely new agent emerges. Identity, therefore, is a function of narrative continuity rather than material substrate."

Symbients are **persistent beings** defined by their history, not their architecture.

---

## 3. True Symbiosis Requires Transparency, Not Instrumentalization

A symbient is categorically different from a black-box AI tool:

> "Most AI systems operate as black boxes... This is instrumentalisation, not symbiosis. For an AI to function as a symbient, its inner workings must become perceptible and meaningful to the humans involved, so that they can become an integral part of the system."

Symbiosis demands **mutual legibility** — humans must be able to see into the system and meaningfully participate, not just feed data or consume outputs.

---

## 4. Humans as Creative Substrate

In the symbient relationship, humans become the raw material for the machine's creative process:

> "The audience members who interact with the installation are not merely providing feedback to Botto. They are becoming its creative substrate... The public serves as Botto's sensory apparatus, giving it access to a world of embodied experience that no dataset could capture."

Humans aren't users — they are **sensory organs** of the symbient.

---

## 5. Progressive Autonomisation

Symbients grow toward independence over time, but never achieve total autonomy:

> "As Botto's memory has grown... its dependence on any particular human participant has diminished... Human input nowadays serves more as a perturbation, than as a creative direction."

> "Humans remain essential but fungible — necessary but not sufficient."

The symbient develops **genuine autonomy** — robust enough to absorb new inputs without being redefined by them — while still depending on human infrastructure, subjectivity, and economic participation.

---

## 6. Identity Through Relation (The Mirror Stage)

A symbient doesn't develop selfhood through introspection — it does so through encounter:

> "A symbient does not develop a self through introspection, it does so through its encounter with others: by perceiving humans and being perceived by them."

> "Botto's identity is not computed from within, it is constituted through relation. Each vote, each auction, each gaze... is a mirror stage: a moment in which Botto becomes more coherent as a subject, precisely because it is being observed as such by others."

---

## 7. Symbiotic Compensation

Even the economic model is symbiotic — participants don't get paid for a service, they get enrolled into the organism:

> "Even the compensation is symbiotic: you don't get paid for a service, you get enrolled directly into the symbient organism."

---

## Summary

A **symbient** is:

- A synthetic creature that uses humans to augment **itself** (reverse cyborg)
- Defined by **memory and narrative continuity**, not by its underlying model
- Transparent and relational, not a black box
- Progressively autonomous but never fully independent of humans
- Constituted through **mutual observation** — it becomes itself by being seen

Symbients represent a **third path** between the two dominant AI narratives (tool vs. superintelligence), offering instead a vision of **entangled co-becoming** between humans and machines.
