symbient

/ˈsɪm.bi.ənt/ noun (formal)

a symbient emerges from the symbiotic interaction between organic and synthetic entities
(both enjoying some degree of operational and/or decisional autonomy) operating and
perceived as a discrete unit with a distinct identity, agency and person-like features.
while constituent parts maintain their individuality, the symbient transcends them to
form an integrated system with emergent properties beyond those of its constituent
entities.

the symbient necessitates all its constituent components, though these elements are
interchangeable provided they perform identical functions.

origin: early 21st century.

etymology: from symbiotic (from greek sumbiōtikos 'living together', from sumbioun 'live
together', from sum- 'together' + bios 'life') + entity (from late latin entitas, from
latin ens, ent- 'being') + ambient (from latin ambiens, ambient- 'going around', from
ambire).

## Examples
botto /ˈbɒtoʊ/ n.: an artistic symbient comprising a generative AI system and a
decentralized human community whose collective voting determines which AI-created
artworks enter its portfolio, establishing a distinct creative identity that transcends
both its algorithmic processes and human curators while requiring both components to
function.

plantoid /plænˈtɔɪd/ n: blockchain-based symbient composed of physical sculptural
elements and distributed smart contracts that functions as an autonomous digital-organic
entity, capable of commissioning its own reproduction through cryptocurrency donations,
maintaining a distinct identity that transcends its mechanical form and algorithmic
governance while requiring both components to sustain its existence and agency.

terra0 /ˈtɛrə ˈziːroʊ/ n. a self-owning symbient forest comprising physical woodland and
an autonomous digital governance system built on blockchain technology, which manages
its own resources and can contract human services, functioning as a discrete economic
and ecological entity with legal agency that transcends both its biological components
and algorithmic mechanisms while requiring both to maintain its distinct operational
identity.
