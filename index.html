Symbient /ˈsɪm.bi.ənt/ noun (formal)

A Symbient is a distinct entity emerging from the symbiosis between organic beings and synthetic systems. A Symbient displays operational and decisional autonomy.

Etymology

From symbiotic (from Greek sumbiōtikos 'living together', from sumbioun 'live together', from sum- 'together' + bios 'life') + entity (from late Latin entitas, from Latin ens, ent- 'being') + ambient (from Latin ambiens, ambient- 'going around', from ambire). Origin: early 21st century.

About

We are building toward new forms of living together with non-human entities.

A symbient is an inverse cyborg: while cyborgs are humans augmenting themselves with technological artefacts, symbients are technological artefacts augmenting themselves with organic entities (humans or non-humans).
